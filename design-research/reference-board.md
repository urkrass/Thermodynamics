- Motion should clarify material behavior, not decorate every element.
- A tiny palette can feel premium when contrast is decisive.
- Short interface labels outperform explanatory clutter.
- Interactive objects need stable surrounding typography.

Three simulation takeaways:
- Make Gibbs energy feel like a live balance, not a stock illustration.
- Use one accent color for feasibility and one neutral field for calculation.
- Keep controls quiet so the scientific scene carries the sense of motion.
